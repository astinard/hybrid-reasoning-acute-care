# NSF Smart Health (SCH) Awards in Clinical AI - 2024

Research compiled on 2025-11-30

## Program Overview

The **Smart Health and Biomedical Research in the Era of Artificial Intelligence and Advanced Data Science (SCH)** program is an interagency collaboration between the National Science Foundation (NSF) and National Institutes of Health (NIH). The program supports transformative high-risk, high-reward advances in computer and information science, engineering, mathematics, statistics, behavioral and cognitive research to address pressing questions in the biomedical and public health communities.

### Funding Details

- **Project Duration**: Up to 4 years
- **Total Award Amount**: $1,200,000 ($300,000 per year)
- **Estimated Awards**: 10-16 per year
- **Total Annual Funding**: $15-20 million
- **Latest Solicitation**: NSF 25-542
- **Proposal Deadlines**: October 3 annually (2024, 2025, etc.)

### Research Focus Areas

1. **Cyber-Physical Systems** - Closed-loop or human-in-the-loop CPS systems to assess, treat, and reduce adverse health events
2. **Robotics** - Novel robotic systems with significant computational capability and physical complexity
3. **Biomedical Image Interpretation** - How human pattern recognition and visual search can inform AI image interpretation
4. **Health Disparities and Health Equity** - Data-driven AI/ML models addressing structural and social determinants of health
5. **Clinical Decision Support** - Human-AI systems for clinical decision support
6. **Medical Devices and Sensors** - Data generation, analysis and automation for biomedical devices and consumer devices (IoT)

## Recent Funded Projects (2023-2024)

### 1. Sepsis Detection Using AI - Johns Hopkins University

**Principal Investigator**: Suchi Saria, Associate Professor
**Institution**: Johns Hopkins University
**Departments**: Computer Science, Biostatistics, Health System Informatics, Health Policy and Management
**Funding Source**: NSF Future of Work at the Human-Technology Frontier & NSF SBIR
**Project**: TREWS (Targeted Real-Time Early Warning System)

**Abstract**: Development of an AI-powered platform integrating electronic health records with machine learning to diagnose sepsis cases nearly two hours earlier than traditional methods. The system has demonstrated:
- 18% reduction in sepsis mortality rates across dozens of hospitals
- Average hospital stay reduction by half a day
- 10% reduction in ICU use since deployment in 2023

**Impact**: The research was spun out through NSF SBIR grant into startup Bayesian Health, transforming TREWS from academic innovation into a scalable care augmentation platform capable of tackling critical conditions beyond sepsis.

**Quote**: "Federal funding, including vital support from the NSF, was foundational to the development of TREWS. It allowed us to conduct the kind of long-horizon, high-impact research that isn't immediately profitable but is essential for building technologies that truly improve patient outcomes."

### 2. Trustworthy AI for Healthcare - University of Florida

**Co-Principal Investigator**: Ruogu Fang, Ph.D., Assistant Professor
**Institution**: University of Florida
**Department**: J. Crayton Pruitt Family Department of Biomedical Engineering
**Funding**: $1.2 million NSF grant
**Project**: Building Confidence into AI for Healthcare

**Abstract**: Research focusing on increasing trustworthiness and interpretability of artificial machine learning in healthcare. The project investigates:
- Ways to use AI to diagnose neurodegenerative diseases earlier
- Paradigm shift for explainable AI explaining how and why ML models make predictions
- Addressing critical need as neurodegenerative diseases impact millions and prevalence expected to double over next decade

**Focus**: The team emphasizes that AI research needs to be trustworthy and explainable to make real impact in medicine.

### 3. Medical Imaging Adversarial Robustness

**Award Number**: NSF CAREER Award #2046708
**Title**: Systematic Mitigation of Deep Learning Adversaries in Medical Imaging
**Project Focus**:

**Abstract**: Provides new insights, accurate yet robust AI techniques, and novel strategies to improve the robustness of medical imaging applications. Research adopts holistic approach including:
- Detecting individual adversarial images
- Differentiating adversarial images from different sources
- Rectifying adversarial images
- Determining transferability of robustness across data domains
- Quantifying output uncertainties

**Department**: Computer Science

## Technical Challenges and Research Priorities

### Core Technical Challenges Addressed

1. **Data Generation and Analysis**
   - Effective data generation from biomedical devices (imaging, medical devices)
   - Electronic health records integration
   - Consumer devices and Internet of Things

2. **AI/ML Model Requirements**
   - Deeper understanding of causality in AI/ML models

3. **Robustness and Trustworthiness**

## Proposal Requirements

### Collaboration Requirements
- **Required**: Collaborations with researchers in health application domains
- **Required**: Collaboration Plan for ALL proposals
- **Team Composition**: Research teams must include members with appropriate and demonstrable expertise in major areas involved in work
- **Training**: Projects expected to include students and postdocs

### PI Participation Limits
In each annual competition, an investigator may participate as:
- Principal Investigator (PI)
- Co-Principal Investigator (co-PI)
- Project Director (PD)
- Senior/Key Personnel
- Consultant

**Limit**: No more than TWO proposals submitted per annual deadline. These constraints are strictly enforced.

### Clinical Research Requirements
If project involves clinical research, plans must include:
1. Protection of human subjects from research risks
2. Inclusion of women, minorities, and individuals across lifespan
3. Justification in terms of scientific goals and research strategy

### Eligibility
- **Institutions of Higher Education (IHEs)**: Two- and four-year accredited institutions in the U.S.
- **Non-profit Organizations**: Independent museums, observatories, research laboratories, professional societies in the U.S.

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **High-Risk, High-Reward Focus**: SCH program specifically targets transformative, disruptive innovations rather than incremental improvements

2. **Interdisciplinary Requirement**: Projects must make fundamental contributions to TWO OR MORE disciplines

3. **Real-World Impact**: Emphasis on projects that will reach clinical implementation and improve patient outcomes

4. **Equity and Fairness**: Strong focus on addressing health disparities, bias in AI systems, and ensuring equitable healthcare

5. **Long-Term Support**: 4-year funding horizon enables deep, foundational research not immediately profitable

6. **Clinical Integration**: Required collaboration with health domain experts ensures clinical relevance

7. **Award Conditions**: All awards made after October 1, 2024 subject to updated NSF award conditions compliant with revised OMB Guidance for Federal Financial Assistance (April 2024)
